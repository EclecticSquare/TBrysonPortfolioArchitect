About the Architect

A while ago, I had that moment where the clouds opened, the sun shined and all became clear. I stood in the rays of light and a voice spoke to me and said, “The time for change is now.” I said, “Could you speak up. I really can’t hear you.” With authority and a lot of sass, the voice replied, “Look lady. Enough is enough. Take that leap and make that change. No fear! No hesitation!” Who could argue with that wisdom. Not me. I started building a coding foundation by reading and delving into as many free resources as I could find. Free Code Camp, Khan Academy and YouTube became my classrooms. I would get stuck and want to beat my head against the wall. I would learn something new and I would give myself a high-five. There would suddenly be an “ah-hah!” moment and I would break into my happy dance. Learning was so much fun that I began attending Meetups and finding other groups that shared my passion for code, inluding Women Who Code, Girl Develop It and Newbie Coders. I loved it! I couldn’t wait any longer. I couldn’t continue to say “Hey, maybe next year.” I wasn't getting any younger and the state of education wasn't getting any better. The lack of opportunity for growth, creativity and autonomy, especially for those who were…well, a little different, was stifling. There were a plethora of ideas in my head fighting to get out. They were running out of room. So…I decided take a leap and worry about the net later.



Immediately after graduating from high school, I spent a summer semester in Ecuador, where I matriculated through the academy of skin color affirmation, being blocked from entering the same places as my lesser melanoid compatriots while experiencing the irony of being called a gringo. It was a paradox of being part of a group and not part of a group at the same time. Despite the adversity, I was not detered. I managed to improve my Spanish, increase my knowledge of Latin American history and acquire a love for cultural experiences abroad.

This was followed by four years at Agnes Scott College where I learned the power of sisterhood, teamwork and being a woman. There, I learned that despite the ugly in the world, there are places where I can go and be accepted as I am. I also gained an understanding of what it means to collaborate with and support one another to reach a goal. I finished with a B.A. in Spanish and a minor in Education and went on to teach in an inner-city high school. There I learned that students do amazing things, despite encountering daunting life events. I also learned the joy of the drumline and the pain of losing someone too soon.

Eventually, I went to Cambridge College to get my Masters in Education Administration. I’d like to say I went on to become a mind-blowing principal, turning around a failing school and receiving accolades from all sides but if that was the case, I probably wouldn’t be applying to coding school. Instead, I learned that education was a political game that I wasn’t interested in playing. Thus, my dive into coding.

I graduated from DigitalCrafts in February of 2018. While there I sharpened my skills, built my knowledge and grew as a developer. With the skills that I have acquired and my continued quest to devour all things code, I am ready to take on the next challenge. My pursuit to expand my understanding of frontend and backend web development is on going. The ever changing landscape is intriguing and the learning never stops but life without the act of acquiring new knowledge is no life at all.

I enjoy creating something from nothing or sometimes, something from something. A blank canvas becomes my latest abstract painting or old hangers, cloth and magazines become a multimedia sculpture. Diapers become cakes wrapped in glitter and bows. A writing prompt morphs into my latest poetic masterpiece and strokes on a keyboard bloom into a playground of wonder. It's all building and it all requires attention to detail, working through processes and troubleshooting. Either way, I keep coming back to creating. It is who I am.

Nowadays, you can find me dancing with the command line, finessing code and hunting for solutions on the web. I continue head-on into the learning curve of life, a lifelong learner, ready for the next lesson.
