About the Architect

A while ago, I had that moment where the clouds open, the sun shines through and all becomes clear. I stood in the rays of light and a voice spoke to me and said, “The time for change is now.” I said, “Could you speak up. I really can’t hear you.” With authority and a lot of sass it replied, “Look lady. Enough is enough. Take that leap and make that change. No fear! No hesitation!” I took a few classes and started on FCC and I found that it was so much fun. I would get stuck and want to beat my head against the wall. I would learn something new and I would give myself a high-five. I would suddenly have an “ah-hah!” moment and I would do my happy dance. I loved it! I can’t wait any longer. I can’t continue to say “Hey, maybe next year.” I am not getting any younger and education is not getting any better. There is little opportunity for growth, creativity and autonomy, especially for those who are…well, a little different. There are many ideas in my head fighting to get out. They’re starting to run out of room. So…I have decided take that leap and worry about the net later.

I also enjoy creating something from nothing or sometimes, something from something. A blank canvas becomes my latest abstract painting or old hangers, cloth and magazines become a multimedia art sculpture. Diapers become cakes wrapped in glitter and bows. A writing prompt morphs into my latest poetic masterpiece. It's all building and it all requires attention to detail, working through processes and troubleshooting. I've had to walk away and come back to a piece of work or I've had to trash it all together and start from scratch. Either way, I keep coming back to creating. It is who I am.

Immediately after graduating from high school, I spent a summer semester in Ecuador, where I matriculated through the academy of skin color affirmation, being blocked from entering the same places as my lesser melanoid compatriots while experiencing the irony of being called a gringo. It was a paradox of being part of a group and not part of a group at the same time. Nevertheless, I managed to improve my Spanish, knowledge of Latin American history and acquire a love for cultural experiences abroad . This was followed by four years at Agnes Scott College where I learned the power of sisterhood, teamwork and being a woman. There, I learned that despite the ugly in the world, there are places where I can go and be accepted as I am. I also gained an understanding of what it means to collaborate with and support one another to reach a goal. I finished with a B.A. in Spanish and a minor in Education and went on to teach in an inner city high school. There I learned that students do amazing things, despite encountering daunting life events. I also learned the joy of the drumline and the pain of losing someone too soon.

Eventually, I went to Cambridge College to get my Masters in Education Administration. I’d like to say I went on to become a mind-blowing principal, turning around a failing school and receiving accolades from all sides but if that was the case, I probably wouldn’t be applying to coding school. Instead, I learned that education was a political game that I wasn’t interested in playing. Thus, my dive into coding. I have worked my way through parts of CodeAcademy, Khan Academy, Free Code Camp and more. I continue to learn from all of these places as I continue taking on the learning curve that is life. I am a lifelong learner, ready for the next lessons.
